Situation

Apple's App Store let slip its intention to ban all apps that facilitated the trading of Binary Options, and there was a concern this ban may be adopted by Google's Play Store too. Nadex specialises in exchange traded binary options, with these making up almost 90% of all trades on their platform. Half of these trades are made through their iOS and Android apps.

Though Nadex is a US regulated finance company, and the pricing of their products is completely transparent, thanks to the nature of an open exchange, Apple and Google were taking a sledge hammer approach in their attempt to protect consumers affected by unscrupulous, unregulated operators in this space. Such a ban would have a massive affect on Nadex's revenue and growth prospects, and these affects needed to mitigated immediately.

An emergency meeting was called by the executive team, with members from the entire chain of design and web development present.

The Brief

We were tasked with designing and building Nadex's first mobile browser trading platform, to protect the significant revenue that would be lost, once the apps were removed from their respective stores. As we had no idea when Apple or Google would remove the apps, we worked with the assumption it would happen tomorrow.

My job as lead UX designer on the project was to very quickly:

  • Engage with stakeholders in Chicago to get their design input, and to reassure them with tangible progress.
  • Work with everyone in the team to map the tasks, create shared understanding and identify potential problems.
  • Identify key user tasks we needed to support, and devise a framework for prioritising the support of these tasks. This would become the basis for our iterative release plan.
  • Work with the team to conceive a feasible design and strategy that would enable us to build and release an MVP (as defined by us), and iterate on this frequently.

Action

  1. I gained support for Nadex's first ever Design Sprint. It's collaborative nature, emphasis on speed in reaching shared understanding and the creation of tangible deliverables would be advantageous for evaluating our work quickly, and reassuring stakeholders.
  2. I planned the sprint and devised the 5 day schedule. Our PO blocked out time in the diaries of our team and stakeholders. A software architect, front-end dev, QA, UI, UX (me) and Tech Lead and PO made up the core team.
  3. I facilitated each day of the design sprint, while contributing to the tasks, and kept our stakeholders in Chicago and London in the loop. This not only really reassured them, but they became really engaged with the process. It was not only a very pleasant surprise, but had a hugely positive impact on our designs.
  4. Together with our UI designer we created medium fidelity, testable prototypes of the outcomes from our collaborative sketch and decision making sessions with the rest of the team. This ability totally freed up our developers to concentrate on preparing the back end.
  5. We presented the prototype for an MVP which everyone in the team had had a hand in influencing, and demonstrated exactly how we planned to get there taking into account key user tasks in priority order and development concerns. An unforeseen bonus was that our developers were able to demo a production ready end-to-end code, allowing a Nadex trader to login and see a position and their balances.
  6. I worked with our user researcher and developed a test script so that our prototype could be tested with a number of existing Nadex traders. Client testing started just 12 days into our project.

The Results

Within 3.5 weeks we designed and built a regulatory compliant, tested MVP of our Nadex PWA, which could have been released if necessary. This enabled traders to view their open positions, view their account balances and reduce their exposure on the go.

Within just an additional 3 weeks, traders were able to view a list of predetermined markets, and open new positions. These markets represented around 80% of those traded, and therefore meant Nadex had a feasible alternative to their native apps for a significant proportion of their revenue.

A group of people who had never worked together became a team, showing trust, support and will to achieve the goal within an extremely short space of time

The prototype was tested and validated a number of design decisions, and showed that IA/navigation changes led to certain user tasks performing better than our native apps.
